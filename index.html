Section 03
Question Bank
Use these to open conversations, uncover pain, and guide discovery.
Opening questions
01How are you currently managing scheduling, billing, and clinical notes?
02What software are you using today — and what do you wish it did better?
03How many providers are in your practice?
04What percentage of your time goes to admin vs. patient care?
05How are you tracking patient outcomes and retention right now?
06What does your new patient intake process look like?
07Tell me a bit about your business.
08How did you hear about ChiroHD? What specifically did you hope to learn today?
09How is [current software] failing you?
10What's your patient conversion rate?
11How are you segmenting your patients — and are you finding limitations?
Digging deeper — pain, value & importance
01What specifically?
02Tell me more.
03How long has this been a problem?
04Why do you think that is?
05What is happening there?
06What does that look like?
07Is this already in the works? What's the roadmap on getting this fixed?
08Is that intentional?
09Could you elaborate a little bit?
10Talk to me about... (repeat what they mentioned)
11How are you doing it today?
12Is this something you're looking to prioritize in the future?
13What would you need to make this work?
14It seems like... it sounds like... it looks like... it feels like...
Split tracking
When a prospect is a talker and the conversation gets convoluted — recenter by breaking it into tracks. Give them the power to guide the direction.
→"I heard A, I heard B, and I heard C — which one do you want to talk about first?"
→Lay everything on the table, then: "What is the most important to you right now?"
Playback
Prove you're listening. Repeat back what you heard — in their language or your own words. Direct or indirect.
→"Just to make sure I got it right — you said [summary]. Did I get that right?"
→"It seems like the biggest challenge is [X] — is that accurate?"
Section 04
Objection Handling
Goal: understand the story the prospect is trying to tell by talking with you.
"We already have software."
"Totally understand. If the transition were seamless, what would your ideal platform do that your current one doesn't?"
"Not the right time." / "No budget."
"Makes sense. Quick question — if budget and timing weren't a factor, would ChiroHD solve your top 3 pain points?"
"Sorry, no budget."
"I understand. Quick question — if budget wasn't an issue, would our solution solve your top 3 problems?"
"We're happy with what we have."
"Good to hear. What does your current setup do well? And is there anything you'd change if you could?"
"Send me some info."
"Absolutely — I'll send that over. So I can make it relevant, what's the biggest thing you're trying to improve with your practice management right now?"
Add a new objection
